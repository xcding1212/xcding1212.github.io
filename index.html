I am currently a tenure-track Assistant Professor in the Department of Statistics at the University of California Davis. From 2018-2020, I was a research associate at Duke University working with Hau-Tieng Wu. Before that, I got my PhD in statistics in 2018 from the University of Toronto under the supervision of Jeremy Quastel.

My research lies in the intersection of applied probability, applied mathematics, mathematical statistics and machine learning theory. Currenly, I mainly focus on the following topics: Random Matrix Theory and its statistical applications, statistical optimal transport, non-stationary time series analysis, statistical foundation for algorithms (machine learning and manifold learning) for complex, high dimensional and noisy data, with applications in medical sciences (especially, sleep dynamics and sleep apnea).

Selected works

  • Statistical inference for principal components of spiked covariance matrices, Submitted, with Zhigang Bao, Jingming Wang, and Ke Wang
  • Estimation and inference for precision matrices of nonstationary time series, The Annals of Statistics, with Zhou Zhou
  • Singular vector and singular subspace distribution for the matrix denoising model, The Annals of Statistics, with Zhigang Bao and Ke Wang
  • Spiked separable covariance matrices and principal components, The Annals of Statistics, with Fan Yang
  • A necessary and sufficient condition for edge universality at the largest singular values of covariance matrices, The Annals of Applied Probability, with Fan Yang
